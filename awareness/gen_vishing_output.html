Vishing
 
A caller posing as the bank fraud desk pressures staff to read back a one-time passcode "to stop a fraudulent transaction." The urgency is the trap.
Protect yourself from a Vishing attack
Slow down — a real fraud desk never needs you to rush or read codes aloud.
Pressure, fear and authority are deliberate manipulation cues, not proof.
Hang up and call the bank back on the official number from your card.
A scripted, panicked or "do it now" tone is a red flag — verify first.
