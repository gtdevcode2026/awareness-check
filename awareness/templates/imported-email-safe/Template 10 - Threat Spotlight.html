⚠  Active Threat Tactics — Their Playbook
🎯
Phishing Campaigns Active This Week
Our SOC has detected a surge in targeted phishing attempts impersonating IT support, executive leadership, and trusted SaaS platforms. All staff should exercise heightened vigilance.
🔍  Threat of the Week
CEO Fraud & Executive Impersonation
Attackers craft emails appearing to come directly from senior leadership, requesting urgent wire transfers, credential resets, or sensitive data — bypassing normal approval chains.
01
🎭
Critical BEC / Phishing
Impersonation
Attackers pose as IT support, executives, or trusted vendors to bypass scepticism. Logos, signatures, and voice tone are replicated to appear legitimate.
Verify sender independently →
02
⏰
High Social Engineering
False Urgency
Manufactured pressure — "Act now or lose access" — short-circuits rational thinking, pushing victims to act before pausing to verify.
Slow down. Verify first. →
03
🔗
Medium URL Manipulation
Link Spoofing
Malicious URLs disguised as look-alike domains (e.g. abc-corp.securelogin.net). Always hover to inspect real destinations before clicking.
Hover before you click →
04
📎
Elevated Malware / Ransomware
Weaponised Attachments
Macros in Office docs, scripts in PDFs, and ZIP archives execute malware instantly upon opening. When in doubt — don't open.
Don't open unexpected files →
🛡  Your Defence — Six Actions That Keep You Safe
✦ Immediate Actions — Apply Today All Staff Required
✓
Verify the sender's full email address — not just the display name
✓
Hover over every link and confirm the real domain before clicking
✓
Never open unexpected attachments without confirming the sender
✓
Enable MFA on every account — email, VPN, and all SaaS apps
✓
Slow down — urgency is a red flag, not a reason to act fast
✓
Forward suspicious messages to SOC-support@abc.com immediately
