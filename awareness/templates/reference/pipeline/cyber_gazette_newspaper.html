PHISHING
Invoice-Fraud Surge Hits Finance Teams as Attackers Impersonate Trusted Vendors
By the ABInBev Security Desk · FRIDAY, 13 JUNE 2026
A wave of business-email compromise is targeting finance and procurement staff with near-perfect copies of supplier invoices. Criminals hijack genuine email threads, quietly swap in their own bank details, and pressure employees to pay before month-end closes. Several look-alike domains were registered the same week.
What it means for you
› Verify any change of bank details by phone, using a number you already have — never the one in the email.
› Treat “urgent, pay today” requests as a warning sign; attackers manufacture time pressure on purpose.
› Forward anything that feels off to the security desk before any money moves.
RANSOMWARE
Ransomware Crews Steal First, Encrypt Later — Backups No Longer End the Story
Modern groups copy sensitive files out of the network before locking systems, then threaten to publish them. A clean backup restores your data but does nothing to stop a leak.
PASSWORD & MFA
MFA-Fatigue Attacks Climb: One Reflex Tap Can Surrender Your Account
Attackers who already hold a stolen password flood employees with repeated approval prompts, betting that a busy person will eventually tap Approve. If a login request appears that you did not start, deny it and report it.
