Don’t Take the Bait
 
Phishing messages disguise themselves as the people and brands you trust to steal credentials and data. A few seconds of scrutiny stops most of them — here is what to watch for.
Check the sender. Verify the real address behind the display name before you trust a message.
 
Hover before you click. Look for look-alike or shortened links that don’t match the real site.
Guard your credentials. No genuine IT or bank request asks for your password or MFA code by email.
 
When in doubt, report it. Forward suspicious messages to the SOC instead of deleting them.
